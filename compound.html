Blue Bottle Technologies is proud to introduce the i4G Node B, a fully 3GPP UMTS compliant 4G communications system engineered for situation awareness and integrated into it’s Autonomous Radio Network.

Each node has a maximum range of 32km and is capable of supporting 30 Mbps of wireless bandwidth which can be allocated across various devices within the compound or along the border. Multiple nodes can be configured to automatically inter-mesh : coverage increases by simply adding more nodes.

TRUE MULTIMEDIA BROADCATS MULTICAST SERVICE

Each i4G Node B can connect to multiple devices, video
cameras, sensors and detectors. The i4G network supports
true Multimedia Broadcast Multicast Service (MBMS) so
surveillance feeds are sent on dedicated channels that can
be accessed directly by the Operations Centre and ground
personnel simultaneously.

This allows devolution of operations to commanders on the
ground close to the action as and when required.

SMART VEHICLE INTERFACE

The i4G smart vehicle interface is a unit with multiple
connection options allowing the operator to have available
live video feeds and communications in vehicle that can
also extend out of vehicle via an on-board WiFi node.
The operator is now able to leave the vehicle and still
maintain access to vital data and information.

This unit can also act as a mobile command centre with
an appropriately sized vehicle with sufficient monitors
and communications handsets pre-installed.

HANDHELD INTERFACE

The hand held unit has selected features of the smart
vehicle interface due to obvious restrictions and comes
either as a Panasonic Toughbook with a suitable interface
card, or a sleeve to allow the use of PDA devices such as
Android smart phones, HTC smart phones, iPhones with
the i4G network.

RAPID RANGE EXTENSION

A dynamic multi-node network can utilise vehicle
mounted ARNs providing rapid deployment of extended
range capabilities and still maintain connectivity
to the operations centre.

HIGH SPEED VEHICULAR MOBILITY

i4G has been tested at up to 220km/hr and maintained
reliable handover at speeds of 200km/hr.

A modified version of the i4G has been tested up to 1000km/hr in a terrestrial to aircraft environment.


LOCAL AUTONOMOUS MEDIA CENTER

Due to the self contained nature of the i4G Node B even
in the event of connection failure to the operations centre personnel
on the ground are still able to communicate with each other and monitor video feeds on their in vehicle and hand held systems, allowing continued operations until
the system can be fully restored.
