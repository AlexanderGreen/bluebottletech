PRODUCTS : i4G

The i4G Node B overcomes the problems associated with WCDMA by employing Time Division CDMA.

The maximum capacity of a WCDMA network is governed by the average level of interference between users, as connections are made into the cell the overall level of interference increases, this in turn affects the receive level of interference to every user on that cell. As more subscribers enter the cell those users furthest from the base station receive the highest amount of interference, at some point the interference pushes those distant users below the signal-to-noise threshold of their handsets which then forces them to lose their connection.

This effect is called "cell breathing". With i4G since each end point is allocated dedicated time slots during transmission, interference, and therefore cell breathing, is eliminated.

Each endpoint is allocated dedicated time slots thereby guaranteeing the available bandwidth by endpoint. The allocation of dedicated time slots also allows for the authorisation of other endpoints to view the data within those time slots, allowing multiple endpoints to view the same data simultaneously without increasing the bandwidth usage. A TD-CDMA ARN can operate up to 30Mbps with a constant range of up to 32Km (subject to the local terrain), this range can be further increased by installing the Node B on an aircraft flying over the area to be covered (subject to obstacles between the Node B and the subscriber).
